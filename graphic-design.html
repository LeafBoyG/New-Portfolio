Print & Identity

Graphic Design

A collection of print media, editorial layouts, and brand identity concepts.

01. Editorial Challenge

Wander/Wild

The Brief: Design a double-page layout for a feature article titled "The Silent Fjords: A Kayaker's Guide to Norway." The layout needs to balance a 500-word article, a compelling headline, a pull quote, and high-quality photography.

Focus: Grid systems, text wrapping, drop caps, typographic hierarchy, and managing bleed/margins for the gutter.

View Spread

02. Information Architecture

Paws & Progress

The Brief: An informational tri-fold brochure to hand out at community events. It must explain their mission, detail how to adopt, outline volunteer opportunities, and provide contact info.

Focus: Folding logic (ensuring panels line up correctly), organizing dense information into digestible chunks, and utilizing iconography.

03. Physical Space Challenge

Nocturne Roasters

The Brief: Create the packaging design for their flagship dark espresso blend, "Midnight Oil." The brand identity should be sleek, slightly moody, and modern.

Focus: Working with die-lines, 3D spatial thinking, barcode placement, and understanding how a design wraps around a physical stand-up pouch.

04. Typographic Challenge

Synesthesia

The Brief: Design an 18x24 inch promotional poster and a physical admission ticket stub that captures the energetic, cutting-edge vibe of an electronic music festival.

Focus: Expressive typography, eye-catching color palettes (CMYK vs. RGB), and translating a large-scale design into a small-scale format.

05. Brand Consistency

Vanguard Architects

The Brief: Design a professional stationery suite (letterhead, envelope, and business card) that reflects a high-end architecture firm known for minimalist and brutalist buildings.

Focus: Restraint, alignment, brand consistency across different dimensions, and suggesting physical print finishes like embossed logos or foil stamping.
